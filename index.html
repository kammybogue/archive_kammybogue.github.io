Research

One fundamental problem that remains a challenge to the scientific community is that of star formation â€“ the exact nature of this process is yet to be fully understood. One approach to investigating this field further is to look in more detail at the environments in which stars form, in the Interstellar Medium.

In particular, the impact of magnetic fields, both on the environments of star formation and indeed within astrophysics as a whole, is especially elusive. My research focusses on this area, hoping to shed some light onto the precise effects magnetic fields are having.

I have been working on two near-identical simulations of an isolated flocculent galaxy, following the work of Tress et al 2020. The only difference between these simulations is the presence of a magnetic field. With these two data sets, I can extract and investigate the properties of molecular clouds present in the simulations. By comparing the two cases to each other, I will be able to make conclusions about the impact of magnetic fields on star-forming environments.
